Dr. Jordan Peterson

Canadian psychologist, author, and media commentator.

Here's a time line of Dr. Jordan Peterson's life:

  • 1962: Jordan Peterson is born on June 12th in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.
  • 1984: Peterson receives his B.A. in political science from the University of Alberta.
  • 1985: He earns his B.A. Honors degree in psychology from the same university.
  • 1991: Peterson completes his Ph.D. in clinical psychology from McGill University.
  • 1993: Peterson begins teaching at Harvard University as an assistant professor of psychology.
  • 1998: He moves to the University of Toronto, where he becomes a full professor in the Department of Psychology.
  • 1999: Peterson publishes his first book, "Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief," which explores the psychological foundations of mythology and religion.
  • 2016: Peterson posts a series of YouTube videos criticizing the Canadian government's Bill C-16, which adds gender identity and expression to the list of protected grounds under the Canadian Human Rights Act and the Criminal Code. The videos go viral, and Peterson becomes a controversial public figure.
  • 2017: Peterson publishes "12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos," a self-help book that draws on psychology, philosophy, and his own experiences to provide practical advice for living a meaningful life. The book becomes a bestseller.
  • 2018: Peterson embarks on a world tour to promote his book and give lectures on a range of topics, including identity politics, free speech, and personal responsibility.
  • 2019: Peterson takes a leave of absence from the University of Toronto to focus on his health after struggling with addiction and depression.
  • 2020: Peterson releases his second book, "Beyond Order: 12 More Rules for Life," which continues his exploration of human psychology and provides additional guidance for personal growth and development.
